# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and talent partners evaluating Mahmoud Sabbagh for senior delivery and operations roles — Project Manager, Scrum Master, Product Owner, Program Coordinator, Operations Manager, and Customer Success Operations. They arrive from LinkedIn, a job application, or a referral, usually skimming on limited time, often on mobile. Their job-to-be-done: in under a minute, decide whether Mahmoud is credible, senior, and worth a conversation.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio/résumé site that converts a skim into a callback. It exists to establish credibility fast through quantified outcomes (Meta vendor accuracy 60→85%+, DealerOn ramp time cut ~65% with a perfect 5/5 CSAT, Loomlance taken from concept to live launch), to make the evidence effortless to scan, and to give a recruiter a clear next step (download the résumé, read the Loomlance case study, or make contact). Success = a recruiter leaves convinced and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Precise, composed, decisive. The voice of an operator who drives KPIs, calms high-stakes accounts, and ships under Scrum. Confident without bravado; the numbers carry the swagger. Emotional goal: a hiring manager should feel *reassured* — "this person brings order and results." Three words: structured, credible, results-driven.

## Anti-references

- The AI editorial-magazine template: display-serif (Fraunces/Playfair) headlines + tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows above every section + mono metadata labels + ruled three-column grids. Overused and generic.
- The SaaS hero-metric template: giant gradient numbers in identical cards with no narrative.
- Generic dev-portfolio dark-terminal aesthetic with monospace everywhere — Mahmoud leads people and programs, not codebases; mono would be costume.
- Timid beige/slate "minimalism" that reads as safe and forgettable.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over adjectives.** Every claim is anchored to a number, a client, or an outcome. Show the result, don't describe the trait.
2. **Respect the skim.** The most important proof is legible in the first fold and scannable throughout; depth is available on demand (Loomlance case study), never forced.
3. **Order is the brand.** Structure, rhythm, and restraint demonstrate the operational discipline the content claims — the design itself is a proof point.
4. **One confident signal.** A single committed crimson carries the identity; everything else stays disciplined ink-on-white. Restraint with intent, not by default.
5. **Credible, not flashy.** Motion and polish serve clarity and confidence; nothing decorative undercuts the senior, trustworthy read.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, verified in both light and dark themes. Full keyboard operability with visible focus states. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every animation (crossfade/instant fallback). Semantic landmarks and headings. Respects `prefers-color-scheme` with a persistent manual toggle. No information conveyed by color alone.
